Data Collection Methodology

How this report was compiled, what sources were used, and what assumptions underpin the estimates.

Data Gathering Process

  1. Extracting the top-grossing lists. The most recent top 20 grossing mobile games in the Philippines were extracted from both AppFigures and Sensor Tower, two of the most widely cited mobile app intelligence platforms used by industry analysts and investors. Lists were pulled separately for iOS (App Store) and Android (Google Play), resulting in up to 40 rows per source.
  2. Estimating PH-specific revenue. Both platforms provide global estimated revenue per app. To isolate Philippine revenue, each game's estimated share of Filipino users (as a percentage of its global user base) was applied to the global monthly revenue figure. These user-share percentages are derived from each platform's geo-demographic models, which use download origin, device locale, and IP-based signals.
  3. Identifying publishers and operating offices. Each game's publisher, registered operating office address, and country of incorporation were manually verified using official app store listings, corporate registry filings (e.g., ACRA Singapore, SEC Philippines), and publisher websites. Where a publisher maintains a Philippine local office (e.g., MOONTON in BGC, Garena on 5th Ave Taguig), that address is noted.
  4. Cross-checking top-up store availability. For each game, the available third-party top-up platforms were cross-checked by visiting each store's website (e.g., Codashop PH, UniPin, SEAGM, Razer Gold) and confirming whether the game is listed as a supported title for Philippine customers. "Internal/In-Game" is recorded when the game supports direct purchases via the native app store.
  5. Classifying revenue sources. Each game's monetization model was categorized by examining the app store listing's "Contains Ads" and "In-App Purchases" labels, supplemented by gameplay review. Games flagged with ad revenue have the 0.2x adjustment applied to estimate the spender-only portion of their monthly PH revenue.
  6. Averaging across sources. When both AppFigures and Sensor Tower report data for the same game on the same platform, the "Average of Both" mode takes the mean of both estimates. When only one source reports a game, that figure is used as-is (not halved). This dual-source approach reduces the impact of estimation bias from any single platform.
Important caveats: All revenue figures are estimates derived from third-party analytics platforms, not official publisher disclosures. Actual revenue may differ. The 0.2x ad-revenue multiplier is a simplified assumption — the true spender-to-ad ratio varies by game. Top-up store revenue distribution assumes equal splits, whereas in reality some stores may capture a larger share due to promotional pricing or regional popularity. This report is intended to provide directional insight, not exact figures.

    Why Console & PC Data is Excluded

    Understanding the difference between mobile top-up spending and traditional gaming purchases.

    Different Monetization Models

    This report focuses exclusively on mobile platforms because they represent a fundamentally different spending pattern. Console and PC games in the Philippines primarily follow a one-time purchase model — players buy the game once (physical or digital) and rarely make additional purchases. The "top-up" or "pins" culture that drives recurring revenue is almost entirely a mobile phenomenon.

    📱

    Mobile (Free-to-Play)

    Free to download, monetized through recurring in-app purchases, subscriptions, and virtual currency "top-ups." Players regularly buy diamonds, coins, skins, and battle passes through third-party top-up stores. Revenue is ongoing and recurring.

    🖥️

    Console & PC (Buy-to-Play)

    Upfront purchase price (₱1,500–₱3,500+). Some games have optional DLC or cosmetics, but the vast majority of Philippine console/PC gamers make a single purchase. Top-up stores and pins are not part of this ecosystem.

    For these reasons, console and PC revenue data would not be meaningful in a report focused on the top-up and recurring spending ecosystem. The spending behaviors, distribution channels, and revenue patterns are fundamentally incompatible with the mobile F2P model analyzed here.

    The Philippine Top-Up Ecosystem: The Philippines has one of the most vibrant top-up ecosystems in Southeast Asia. Unlike Western markets where purchases flow almost exclusively through Google Play and the App Store, Filipino gamers frequently use third-party top-up platforms to purchase in-game currency, diamonds, and battle passes — often at discounted rates or via local payment methods like GCash, Maya, and over-the-counter payments at convenience stores (7-Eleven, Ministop) and sari-sari stores.     Why So Many Publishers Are Registered in Singapore

    A significant number of games in this report are published by companies registered in Singapore — even when the actual development team is based in China, South Korea, or elsewhere. This is a deliberate business strategy, not a coincidence.

    Singapore serves as the regional headquarters for Southeast Asian (SEA) operations for many global gaming companies. The reasons include: favorable corporate tax rates (17% headline rate with numerous incentives), strong intellectual property protections, a business-friendly regulatory environment, and strategic proximity to the massive SEA gaming market (700M+ people across ASEAN).

    Companies like Century Games (Kingshot, Whiteout Survival), COGNOSPHERE/HoYoverse (Genshin Impact, Honkai: Star Rail), Level Infinite/Tencent (Honor of Kings), InFold (Love and Deepspace), HABBY (Archero 2), and many others operate their SEA publishing and distribution through Singapore entities — even though their development studios are in mainland China. Similarly, FUNFLY (Last War) and Omnilojo (Last Z) are Singapore-registered entities for games developed by Chinese studios.

    For this report, we classify these publishers by their registered operating office location, which is why Singapore appears as one of the largest revenue sources on the map. This reflects where the business decisions and revenue flows are managed, rather than where the code is written.

    PH Mobile Game Spending Forecast (2026–2030)

    Projected annual growth of Filipino mobile game spending based on current monthly run rate and regional growth trends.

    Forecast Methodology & Key Drivers

    The forecast projects annual PH mobile game spending by annualizing the current monthly run rate (×12) and applying a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 12–15%, consistent with industry analyst projections for the Southeast Asian mobile gaming market. The shaded band represents the range between conservative (12% CAGR) and optimistic (15% CAGR) scenarios.

    Key growth drivers for the Philippines include:

    • Rising smartphone penetration — the Philippines is projected to reach 85%+ smartphone penetration by 2028, up from ~72% in 2025, bringing millions of new potential mobile gamers online.
    • Expanding digital payment infrastructure — GCash surpassed 94 million registered users in 2025 and Maya continues to grow, making in-app purchases and top-ups more accessible, especially in underbanked rural areas.
    • Young demographics — with a median age of ~25, the Philippines has one of the youngest populations in Asia, and gaming engagement peaks in the 15–35 age range.
    • 5G rollout & improving connectivity — major telcos Globe and Smart are expanding 5G coverage, enabling higher-quality mobile gaming experiences that drive higher spending per user.
    • Esports ecosystem growth — MPL Philippines, CODM tournaments, and Free Fire leagues drive competitive engagement, which strongly correlates with higher in-game spending.
    • Third-party top-up ecosystem — the deep penetration of platforms like Codashop, UniPin, and Creamstore.ph reduces friction for purchases, unlike markets that rely solely on app store billing.

    Risk factors: Potential regulatory changes on microtransactions, economic slowdowns affecting disposable income, and market saturation in the MOBA/battle royale genres could slow growth below the conservative estimate.
